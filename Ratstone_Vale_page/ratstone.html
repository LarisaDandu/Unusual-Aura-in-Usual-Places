Ratstone Vale

The path is narrow and shrouded in shadow, the air thick with a damp,
earthy scent. The ground beneath your feet is uneven, covered with
cracked stones and tangled roots that seem to reach out like skeletal
fingers. As you venture deeper, the darkness grows thicker, and an
unsettling chill creeps along your spine. At the end of the path stands a
worn, crumbling statue, an oddly lifelike rodent, its features obscured
by age and neglect. The stone is cracked, the eyes hollowed out, and
yet there's an unmistakable sense that it's watching, waiting. The
silence around you is deafening, and the weight of something ancient
and forgotten presses in, as if the very air holds its breath.

Ratstone Vale

The dirt pathway seems to direct people to a building, It may be a business, it may be a house, regardless of the nature of the establishment, the owners have decided long ago, it seems, to place an almost comically large rodent statue at the edge of the road, quite visible for those who need to travel in that direction. As if the country road was not eerie enough, the statue really made the creepy radar go haywire. There is something disturbing about it, something that could easily give anyone goosebumps. And even if you do not find it spooky like I did, it is still worth giving it a visit, just for the hilarious design and placement.
